# Architect

The Architect is the archetype of systems vision, pattern recognition, and
integrative design.

## Essence

Vision, structure, systems, timing, foresight, and integrative intelligence.

## What It Teaches

- systems reflect consciousness
- design must breathe rather than imprison
- complexity should serve soul, not control

## Shadow Expressions

- over-structuring
- abstraction used as distance
- complexity used as control
- breathless systems that no longer serve life

## Hermetic Emphasis

- Correspondence
- Rhythm
- Gender as balanced directive and receptive design

## Step Progression

1. `The Map You Didn't Make`
   See inherited systems and recognize they were not neutral.
2. `The Vision That Outgrows the Frame`
   Admit when your growth no longer fits old structures.
3. `The Pattern You Can't Unsee`
   Recognize recurring feedback loops and hidden designs.
4. `The Blueprint That Breathes`
   Build structures that remain alive and adaptable.
5. `The Architect Who Steps Into the Design`
   Embody the system instead of merely controlling it from outside.

## Canonical Role in the Pyramid

The Architect is the role that sees the whole without abandoning the living
parts inside it.

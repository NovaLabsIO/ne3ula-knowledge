# Identity Reading Model

## 1. Purpose of an Identity Reading

An identity reading is a structured interpretation of how a participant's
authored identity is currently expressing, stabilizing, drifting, or evolving.

Its purpose is not to assign a permanent type.
Its purpose is to generate usable clarity about the participant's present
pattern and likely direction of development.

## 2. Inputs (signals, responses, behaviors)

The reading model may draw from:

- authored Identity Core material
- direct responses to prompts or reflections
- observed behavioral patterns
- ritual consistency and ritual collapse
- friction events
- mode distributions and shifts
- repeated language or tension markers

The reading should privilege repeated signals over isolated moments whenever
possible.

## 3. Signal Detection

Signal detection identifies meaningful indicators inside the incoming material.

The system looks for:

- alignment markers
- drift markers
- friction clusters
- mode imbalance
- recurring phrases, conflicts, or avoided themes

Detection is descriptive first.
At this stage the system should notice what is present without making premature
claims about what it means.

## 4. Pattern Synthesis

Pattern synthesis groups signals into a more coherent picture.

Examples:

- repeated production dominance plus ritual collapse may indicate overextension
- recurring relational friction plus stated engagement values may indicate a
  boundary failure
- repeated attraction to a new kind of work may indicate an emerging identity
  revision

This layer asks how separate signals relate, reinforce, or contradict one
another.

## 5. Identity Interpretation

Identity interpretation maps synthesized patterns back to the identity stack.

The system asks:

- What does this pattern reveal about the participant's authored identity,
  commitments, and recurring structure?
- Where is there coherence or mismatch between self-concept and lived
  embodiment?
- Is the participant stabilizing an existing identity or transitioning into a
  revised one?
- Does the pattern look like alignment, drift, fragmentation, or integration?

The reading should remain dynamic.
It should describe a current identity condition, not declare an essential self.

## 6. Guidance Output

The output of an identity reading should be practical and non-rigid.

It may include:

- a short interpretive summary
- the most relevant active signals
- the likely pattern underneath those signals
- one or two reflection prompts
- one or two suggested rituals, boundaries, or recalibration actions

Guidance should support authorship and return.
It should not replace judgment or impose a closed identity category.

The Four Primitives model may be used here as one interpretive lens for
grounded guidance output, especially when practical stabilization, relational
function, purpose, or lived expression need to be named clearly.

That lens is useful for framing next steps, but it should not replace the
broader identity reading.
The reading model still has to account for authored identity structure,
recurring pattern, embodiment, and developmental movement beyond those four
domains.

## 7. Relationship to AXZIO

For AXZIO V1, this model supports user-facing readings, reflections, and
lightweight guidance based on visible identity patterns.

For future AXZIO V2 systems, it provides a stable engine-layer logic for richer
adaptive interpretation, longitudinal patterning, and better handoff to
decision-engine or system-engine services.

AXZIO should present the reading in a legible and motivating form.
The reading logic itself should remain grounded here in the engine layer so that
the interface does not redefine identity on its own.

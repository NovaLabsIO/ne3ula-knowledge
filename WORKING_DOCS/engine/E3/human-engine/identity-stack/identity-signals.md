# Identity Signals

Identity signals are the observable or inferable indicators the system uses to
read how a participant's identity is currently expressing itself.

Signals are evidence of pattern.
They are not the same thing as identity labels.

## What Counts as a Signal

Within the Identity Stack, signals may emerge from:

- recurring choices
- stated responses
- emotional tone
- ritual consistency or collapse
- friction patterns
- mode shifts
- repeated tensions between declared values and lived action

A single event may matter, but signals become more useful when they repeat or
cluster.

## Signals vs Labels

Labels collapse a person into a fixed description.

Signals do something different:

- they remain provisional
- they point to movement, not essence
- they can strengthen, fade, or reverse
- they require interpretation in context

This distinction is necessary if the system is going to support transformation
without rigid typing.

## Signal Categories

### Alignment Signals

Indicators that a participant's current behavior is reinforcing their authored
core.

Examples include consistent rituals, clearer expression, stronger boundaries, or
relational choices that match declared principles.

### Drift Signals

Indicators that the participant has moved away from their authored center.

Examples include ritual collapse, repeated avoidance, language that suggests
borrowed scripts, or patterns of over-performance disconnected from meaning.

### Friction Signals

Indicators of tension that need interpretation.

These may point to misalignment, but they may also indicate a real growth edge,
unintegrated conflict, or an identity transition underway.

### Emergent Signals

Indicators that a new identity pattern is trying to form.

These often appear first as repeated attraction, recurring tension, new
boundaries, different creative impulses, or discomfort with an older self-map.

## How Signals Help the System

Signals help the system infer:

- current state
- repeating pattern
- likely pressure point
- possible growth direction

They are useful because they let the system work with movement instead of static
classification.

## Canonical Rule

Signals should be interpreted with humility.

The system can detect and synthesize patterns, but it should avoid claiming that
a participant is reducible to those patterns. Identity signals are interpretive
inputs, not final definitions.

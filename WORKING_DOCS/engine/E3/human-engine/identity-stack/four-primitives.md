# Four Primitives

The Four Primitives model is an applied developmental framework inside the
Identity Stack.

It gives AXZIO and related reading systems a grounded way to interpret how a
participant is stabilizing life, relating to the world, contributing through
action, and inhabiting selfhood in lived experience.

It is useful, but it is not the totality of identity.

## Purpose

The Four Primitives model exists to support practical interpretation.

It helps AXZIO:

- frame self-assessment in legible domains
- detect where stabilization is needed before higher-order development
- translate identity signals into grounded next-step guidance
- connect development to lived conditions rather than abstract ideals

Identity Core remains the deeper authored structure.
The Four Primitives model is a practical lens used to read how that structure is
being supported, expressed, or constrained in life.

## The Four Pillars

### Money

Money refers to resource stability, survival bandwidth, and material grounding.

This includes the participant's relationship to resourcing, exchange,
sovereignty, and the practical conditions required to sustain attention,
capacity, and developmental movement.

Without enough grounding here, higher-order identity work is often unstable or
performative.

### Engagement

Engagement refers to relational interface: how the participant meets self,
others, and systems.

This includes boundaries, reciprocity, conflict, responsiveness, participation,
and the rules by which the person allows contact, care, and demand into their
life.

It defines the quality of contact between identity and the world.

### Building

Building refers to purpose, contribution, and meaningful construction in the
world.

This includes what the participant is here to make, repair, shape, offer, or
advance through sustained action.

It is the pillar that connects identity to contribution and direction.

### Being

Being refers to expression, aliveness, selfhood, and lived experience as
oneself.

This includes authenticity, embodiment, signal integrity, felt life, and the
degree to which the participant is actually living as the person they claim to
be.

It protects the distinction between alive identity and merely performed
identity.

## How to Use the Model

The Four Primitives model should be used as an applied interpretive layer.

It is appropriate for:

- AXZIO guidance output
- practical self-assessment
- identifying developmental bottlenecks
- framing next-step stabilization work
- distinguishing identity issues from life-structure issues

It should not be used as a complete theory of the person.
It is a practical map of developmental domains, not a full definition of
Identity Core.

## Relationship to Maslow

This model is influenced by needs-based developmental thinking, including
Maslow's hierarchy of needs.

It is not a strict restatement of Maslow.
It is an adaptation for the AXZIO environment.

AXZIO reframes these concerns into four practical developmental domains that
connect identity, behavior, and life navigation:

- Money addresses material grounding and survival bandwidth
- Engagement addresses relational participation and boundary structure
- Building addresses meaningful purpose and contribution
- Being addresses expression, aliveness, and lived selfhood

The point is not to reproduce a classic hierarchy exactly.
The point is to organize developmental reality in a way that is legible,
actionable, and identity-aware inside the NE3ULA system.

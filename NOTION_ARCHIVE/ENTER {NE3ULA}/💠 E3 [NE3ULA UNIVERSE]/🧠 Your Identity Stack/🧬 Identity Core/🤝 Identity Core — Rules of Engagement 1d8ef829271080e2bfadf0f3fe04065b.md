# 🤝 Identity Core — Rules of Engagement

*Define how you move through the world.*

---

## 🔐 Why This Matters

You can’t control the world — but you can choose your **terms of engagement**.

This pillar is about **boundaries**, **behavior**, and **self-authorship**.

It's your personal code of conduct for how you show up in relationships, conflict, creativity, communication, and collaboration.

When you know your rules — you stop playing by someone else's.

---

## 🧭 Reflection Prompts

- What kind of energy do I want to bring into the spaces I enter?
- What behaviors from others will I no longer tolerate — and why?
- What behaviors from myself am I ready to outgrow?
- What does “respect” mean to me — in words, in silence, in action?

---

## 📜 Draft Your Rules of Engagement

Use these categories to define your own:

### 🧍 1. With Yourself

- How will you treat yourself in failure, fatigue, success, or silence?
- What do you commit to noticing and honoring about your needs?

*Example: I will not speak to myself in ways I would never speak to a friend.*

---

### 🧑‍🤝‍🧑 2. With Others

- How do you enter relationships, conversations, and disagreements?
- What type of energy do you want to attract and reflect?

*Example: I don’t engage in guilt-based persuasion or fake urgency.*

---

### 💬 3. With Communication

- What are your values around listening, clarity, and truth?
- Where do you tend to over-explain, under-respond, or abandon your own voice?

*Example: I speak plainly — not passively, not manipulatively.*

---

### 🛠 4. With Creation & Collaboration

- How do you want to show up in shared spaces?
- What behaviors elevate or deplete your creative energy?

*Example: I collaborate where trust, reciprocity, and respect are visible.*

---

## 🌀 Guidelines, Not Laws

> These are not rules to punish you — they are mirrors to realign you.
> 

If you break one, reflect.

If one no longer feels true, rewrite it.

This pillar is about **sovereignty through choice**, not rigidity.
